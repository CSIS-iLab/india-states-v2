**States Weekly Newsletter**

**April 22, 2026**

**Big News: Chhattisgarh** launches the **‘Atal Arogya Lab’ (Atal Health Lab); Bihar** issues a draft **Bihar Green Hydrogen Policy, 2026; Uttar Pradesh** approves **revised minimum wage rates across the state; Maharashtra** issues a **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for outdoor informal workers in urban areas during summer; Meghalaya** declares **Khasi and Garo languages as official languages, alongside English;** and more.

**Health** 

**Bihar** imposes a complete ban on **private practice by doctors employed by government hospitals**.​

**Source:** [Morung Express](https://morungexpress.com/bihar-bans-private-practice-of-government-doctors#google_vignette)

**In detail:** [Government order](https://state.bihar.gov.in/health/cache/19/16-Apr-26/SHOW_DOCS/319%283%29-11.04.2026.pdf)

**Chhattisgarh** launches the **‘Atal Arogya Lab’ (Atal Health Lab).** The scheme aims to strengthen diagnostic healthcare infrastructure by providing free testing facilities to citizens. 133 types of diagnostic tests will be offered at no cost in 1,046 government healthcare institutions including District Hospitals, Community Health Centers (CHCs), and Primary Health Centers (PHCs).

**Source:** [Daily Hunt](https://m.dailyhunt.in/news/india/english/newsmill-epaper-newsmil/chhattisgarh+cm+vishnu+deo+sai+launches+atal+arogya+lab+in+bastar+region-newsid-n708355899)

**In detail:** [Press Release](https://dprcg.gov.in/post/1776094970/Raipur-New-beginning-of-a-health-revolution-in-Bastar-State-level-Launch-of-%E2%80%98Atal-Aarogya-Lab%E2%80%99-in-Sukma)

**Climate Change and Energy**

  
**Delhi** issues **(DERC) Delhi Electricity Regulatory Commission (Terms and Conditions for Green Energy Open Access) (First Amendment) Regulations, 2026**. The regulations, issued by the Delhi Electricity Regulatory Commission (DERC) remove the requirement that consumers be connected at 11 kV or above to qualify for green energy open access.

**Source:** [Mercom](https://www.mercomindia.com/delhi-proposes-expanding-green-energy-open-access-eligibility)

**In detail:** [Delhi Electricity Regulatory Commission (Terms and Conditions for Green Energy Open Access) (First Amendment) Regulations, 2026](https://www.derc.gov.in/sites/default/files/Proposed%20First%20Amendment%20GEOA_0.pdf)

**Bihar** issues a draft **Bihar Green Hydrogen Policy, 2026**. The policy imposes a five-year roadmap for the state to build a strong ecosystem for the production and use of green hydrogen and green ammonia. The policy follows a draft policy published in 2025.

**Source:** [Solar Quarter](https://solarquarter.com/2026/04/17/bihar-draft-green-hydrogen-policy-2026-targets-1-lakh-tonnes-production-and-%E2%82%B916000-crore-investment/)

**In detail:** [Bihar Green Hydrogen Policy 2026](https://breda.co.in/Uploads/NoticeBoard/DraftGreenHydrogen_Policy2026.pdf)

**Madhya Pradesh** issues a **Fifth Amendment to (MPERC) Cogeneration and Generation of Electricity from Renewable Sources of Energy (Revision-II Regulations 2021 \[RG-33(II) of 2021\]**. The regulations issued by the Madhya Pradesh Electricity Regulatory Commission (MPERC) details new rules to promote clean power and improve compliance across the state.

**Source:** [Solar Quarter](https://solarquarter.com/2026/04/15/mperc-notifies-fifth-amendment-sets-higher-renewable-purchase-targets-till-2030-in-madhya-pradesh/)

**In detail:** [Fifth Amendment](https://mperc.in/uploads/notice/97e6317a9beca2dfab2a5625119bdd6a.pdf)

**Chhattisgarh** issues a **Framework for Resource Adequacy Regulations, 2026.** The framework, issued by the Chhattisgarh State Electricity Regulatory Commission (CSERC), establishes a comprehensive planning mechanism to integrate renewable energy and battery storage to ensure a reliable electricity supply.

**Source:** [Mercom](https://www.mercomindia.com/chhattisgarh-mandates-10-year-resource-planning-framework-for-power-sector)

**In detail:** [Framework for Resource Adequacy Regulations, 2026](https://cserc.gov.in/upload/upload_regulation/26-03-2026_17745192231.pdf)

**Telangana** issues **(TERC) Third Amendment to the Electricity Supply Code Regulation, 2026.** The amendment, issued by the Telangana Electricity Regulatory Commission (TERC), aims to revise the process for handling billing complaints and to make it more structured and consumer friendly.

**Source:** [Solar Quarter](https://solarquarter.com/2026/04/20/terc-drafts-third-amendment-to-electricity-supply-code-regulation-2026-to-ensure-fair-billing-and-consumer-protection-in-telangana/)

**In detail:** [Public Notice](https://www.eqmagpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Public-Notice_compressed.pdf)

**Industrial Policy and Business Regulations**  

**Punjab** allows **landowners to undertake desilting of rivers, small streams, and drains at their own cost.** Under the program, landowners can use excavated material free of charge.​

**Source:** [Hans India](https://www.thehansindia.com/news/national/punjab-govt-allows-landowners-to-desilt-rivers-choes-1066671)

**Goa** notifies the **Goa (Verification of Tenants) Rules, 2026.** The rules introduce a statewide system that makes it compulsory for property owners to verify the identity of their tenants and submit their details to the local police station within five days of occupation.

**Source:** [The Goan](https://www.thegoan.net/goa-news/state-govt-notifies-new-tenant-verification-rules/146710.html)

**In detail:** [Goa (Verification of Tenants) Rules, 2026](https://goaprintingpress.gov.in/downloads/2627/2627-3-SI-OG.pdf)

**Land Acquisition and Labor Regulations**  

**Uttar Pradesh** approves **revised minimum wage rates across the state.** The new wage regime, effective April 1, 2026, will include a Variable Dearness Allowance (VDA) along with the basic wage.

**Source:** [Moneycontrol](https://www.moneycontrol.com/news/india/uttar-pradesh-clears-new-minimum-wages-3-tier-structure-from-april-1-13892477.html)

**In detail:** [Labour department notification](https://uplabour.gov.in/MediaGallery/Circulars/document-34.pdf)

**Punjab** launches the ‘**30 Din Takseem’ (30 Day Division) scheme**. The scheme is designed to resolve long-standing family agricultural land disputes within a 30-day window.

**Source:** [The Statesman](https://www.thestatesman.com/india/cm-mann-launches-30-din-takseem-land-reform-and-issues-a-warning-to-striking-commission-agents-1503583253.html)

**Infrastructure and Governance**

**Maharashtra** issues a **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for outdoor informal workers in urban areas during summer.** The SOP mandates rescheduling of working hours and a compulsory break from 12 noon until 4pm during orange and red alerts about heat. The SOP will apply to all municipal corporations and councils in high-risk urban areas across the state, including Mumbai.

**Source:** [Hindustan Times](https://www.hindustantimes.com/cities/mumbai-news/heatwave-sop-mandates-12-4pm-break-for-informal-workers-101776107475948.html#google_vignette)

**Chhattisgarh** announces a **50 percent reduction in property registration charges and a 50 percent concession in property registration fees for assets registered in the name of women**.

**Source:** [ANI](https://www.aninews.in/news/national/politics/chhattisgarh-cabinet-initiates-ucc-push-50-pc-property-registration-relief-for-women20260415174659/)

**In detail:** [Cabinet Decision](https://dprcg.gov.in/post/1776249859/Raipur-Cabinet-Meeting-Date-15-April-2026)

**Jharkhand** approves a revised policy to **formalize unauthorized construction**. The policy will apply to commercial and non-commercial buildings of three floors total constructed in a 358 square yard (300 square meter) area plot.

**Source:** [Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/ranchi/building-regularisation-plan-jharkhand-approves-regularisation-for-illegal-buildings-policy-aims-to-impact-five-lakh-households-and-boost-state-revenue/articleshow/130315692.cms)

**In detail:** [Cabinet Decision](https://cm.jharkhand.gov.in/sites/default/files/cabinet-decisions-2026-04/cabinet_decision-15-04-2026%28Hindi%29.pdf)

**Meghalaya** declares **Khasi and Garo languages as official languages, alongside English**. The two languages will now be officially used in all government communication and in the state legislature.

**Source:** [Assam Tribune](https://assamtribune.com/north-east/meghalaya-cabinet-declares-khasi-garo-as-state-languages-repeals-2005-act-1610687)

**In detail:** [Press Release](https://meghalaya.gov.in/press/content/50575)

**Punjab** announces the **‘Mukh Mantri Mawan Dheeyan Satkar Yojna’ (Chief Minister's Scheme for Honoring Beloved Daughters).** Under the scheme, women from the general category will receive monthly financial assistance of USD 10.70 (INR 1,000) while women from Scheduled Castes will get USD 16.05 (INR 1,500).

**Source:** [The Pioneer](https://dailypioneer.com/news/punjab-cm-launches-monthly-assistance-scheme-for-women)

**In detail:** [Mukh Mantri Mawan Dheeyan Satkar Yojna](https://acrobat.adobe.com/id/urn:aaid:sc:VA6C2:1862c28d-084a-4275-8e82-b0c7b8fa73c7)

**Water and Sanitation**

**Uttar Pradesh** launches the **‘Jal Pakhwada’ (Water Fortnight Sessions)**. These sessions, focusing on water conservation, will be organized in all schools and educational institutions across the state from April 16 to 30, 2026.

**Source:** [Hindustan Times](https://www.hindustantimes.com/cities/lucknow-news/15day-water-conservation-campaign-begins-in-up-schools-today-101776283269604.html)

**In detail:** [Press Release](https://www.newsonair.gov.in/uttar-pradesh-launches-jal-pakhwada-in-schools-to-promote-water-conservation/)
